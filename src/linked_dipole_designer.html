Build Notes

  • Cut each leg long by a few inches initially and trim symmetrically while checking SWR/resonance with an antenna analyzer — these are calculated starting points, not exact tuned lengths.
  • The apex angle and wire-end height are computed from your two support heights (and rope length, if used) — if your center support is much taller than the ends, the antenna droops more and the correction factor shifts accordingly.
  • Links should be placed at the calculated section boundaries, using low-loss connectors (rings, snap clips, or bullet connectors).
  • If your two legs use different end-support heights or rope lengths, run each leg through the tool separately.
The core calculation — quarter-wavelength from the speed of light with an explicit velocity factor, plus an apex-angle correction table for inverted-V geometry — Always confirm final lengths with an antenna analyzer before trusting the antenna in the field. — KD4CTJ
